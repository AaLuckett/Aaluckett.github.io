Foundations Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

  • I've learned that my ego can sometimes hinder my progress in programming. It's essential to be open to the idea that making mistakes is part of the learning process.
  • I've discovered that I have a tendency to rush through coding challenges to prove my abilities, which can lead to errors and missed learning opportunities. Slowing down is crucial.
  • I've realized that patience and resilience are key qualities in programming. It's okay to face difficulties and setbacks; the important thing is to keep trying and learning from them.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

  • Values guide my decision-making in programming.
  • Empathy helps me understand/collaborate effectively with team members.
  • Self-awareness is crucial in recognizing my strengths and weaknesses. It allows me to focus on areas where I need improvement and seek help or resources when necessary.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

  • I was surprised by how much personal growth and self-reflection are intertwined with programming. It's not just about writing code but also about understanding myself and working effectively with others.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

  • The most challenging aspect was confronting my own ego and accepting that I don't have to know everything. It can be humbling to ask for help or admit when you don't understand something fully.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

  • Focusing on core learning in a programming school is essential because it builds a strong foundation. These skills will help individuals not only become better programmers but also better team members and problem solvers.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

  • The time spent on core learning is not a waste but a valuable investment. While programming skills are essential, core learning enhances one's ability to apply those skills effectively. It's a balance that ultimately makes you a more well-rounded and successful developer.
