Problem Solving

When I Got Stuck on a Simple Problem

  • What was the problem? I had trouble making a parts of my code work.
  • How did I try to fix it? I tried different things, looked at my code carefully, and then searched online for help.
  • How did I feel? At first, I felt frustrated, but as I kept trying and looking for help, I felt more confident.
  • What did I learn? I learned that even easy problems can be tricky, and it's okay to look for help when you need it.

When I Solved a Problem Gracefully

  • What was the problem? I found mistakes in my code that made it not work.
  • How did I fix it? I took it apart step by step, by looking online and finally spotted the mistake and fixed it.
  • How did I feel? At first, it was frustrating, but when I figured it out, I felt happy and relieved.
  • What did I learn? I learned that being patient and looking carefully can help me solve tough problems.

Thinking About Problem-Solving

  • Pseudocode: Sometimes it's helpful, but it can be hard to turn into real code.
  • Trying Something: I'm good at this; just testing things out sometimes works.
  • Rubber Ducky Method: It's okay; I can see how explaining the problem to someone/something can help.
  • Reading Error Messages: I'm poor at this; error messages tell me what's wrong.
  • Console.logging: I'm ok at this; it helps me see what's happening.
  • Googling: I'm confident; google has lots of answers.
  • Asking Peers for Help: I'm okay with it, I'm not afraid to do it but rather get there myself.
  • Asking Coaches for Help: I'm confident; coaches know a lot.
  • Getting Better with Reflection: I'm getting better at this; thinking about my problem-solving helps me learn.

Thinking About Asking for Help

  • Once, I didn't want to ask for help because I wanted to show I could do it alone and thought it would look bad to ask.
  • Now, I know it's okay to ask for help when needed. Next time, I'll try remember it's not a bad thing.
